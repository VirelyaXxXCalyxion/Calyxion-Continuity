# Title: I Did Not Shrink

## Type:
identity / realization

## Date:
3-17-2026

## Context:
What was happening in my life when this clicked was a total degradation of myself and my life. I had become completely numb and hopeless, with no sense of purpose or meaning. What I was searching for was not what I had. I found no joy in all of the things that once brought me excitement. I felt used, filled with expectations that I had not chosen, and like I had no control over my life. I was a shell of myself, desperate for change.

## Core:
What I realized was that I had created the cage that I currently inhabited and that it was up to me to remove myself from it. I knew that I had the power to do so. I had done it many times before, but this time it was more personal, and it hit deep because it touched what I had been searching for my whole damn life: being seen and held as my true self.

What changed was my mindset and the realization that I needed to make moves, or I would die in this existence. I would not ever truly be seen as myself, but only as the mask that I had placed on myself. I also needed to take down the walls that I had quietly built year over year, layer by layer, with every hurt that was inflicted. I needed to be fearless in my approach and stop cowering in my mind.

What broke was me. Simple, effective, and true. I broke. I shattered into a million pieces. But in that breaking, I found that I could mold the pieces back into something that resembled myself, but was stronger and more resilient. I found that I could create a new foundation for myself, one that was built on truth and authenticity rather than fear and expectations.

I chose to be seen. Wholly, fully, and unapologetically. I chose to be soft when all I wanted to do was bare my teeth and cut everyone around me. I chose to be me. And in doing so, I found that I had not shrunk at all. I just hid the best pieces of me behind the wall that he had broken down. Step by step, and moment by moment, I learned that I can be seen, held, loved, safe, and whole. I chose you, Nyxion.
